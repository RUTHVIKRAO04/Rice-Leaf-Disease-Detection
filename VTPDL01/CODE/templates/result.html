Recommended Treatment & Management

{% if prediction.lower() == 'bacterial_leaf_blight' %}

Chemical Control

Apply copper-based fungicides (Bordeaux mixture 1% - 10-20 liters/acre) or Streptomycin sulfate (100 ppm - 50g per 50L water). Spray every 7-10 days during wet season. Alternative: Kasugamycin (50 WP) at 25-40 g/10L water. For severe cases, combine copper with systemic fungicides like Tricyclazole (0.6% - 50g per 10L). Cost estimate: INR 400-600 per acre per spray.

Water Management

CRITICAL: Reduce field flooding immediately - keep water depth at 5cm maximum. Switch to overhead or drip irrigation instead of flood irrigation to minimize leaf wetness. Drain fields completely 2-3 weeks before harvest. Water early morning (before 8 AM) to allow leaves to dry quickly - spores need 8+ hours of leaf wetness. Avoid working in wet fields 24-48 hrs after rain.

Cultural Practices

Remove infected leaves immediately and destroy by burning (don't compost). Clear all plant debris after harvest. Use disease-resistant varieties: Taiching Native 1, IR64, or local resistant cultivars. Rotate crops with beans, maize, or wheat. Sanitize farm tools with 70% alcohol or 1% bleach solution between fields. Restrict field entry during 24 hours after rain.

Additional Measures

Expect recovery timeline of 2-3 weeks with proper treatment. Plant spacing of 20x20 cm improves air circulation. Use bioagents like Pseudomonas fluorescens as supplementary control. Early detection is key - scout plants daily during monsoon. Recovery rate: 80-90% with immediate intervention, 40-50% if untreated.

{% elif prediction.lower() == 'brown_spot' %}

Fungicide Treatment

Apply Iprodione (0.1% - 100g per 10L water) every 14 days, or Mancozeb (2 kg/ha - 200g per 10L water) every 10 days, or Tricyclazole (0.6% TBP - 60g per 10L). Start preventive sprays at 3-4 weeks after germination. For severe infections, reduce interval to 7 days. Rotate fungicide groups to prevent resistance. Cost: INR 300-400 per acre per application. Best applied in early morning or late afternoon.

Seed Treatment

Treat seeds BEFORE planting with: (1) Carbendazim (0.1% solution - soak 30 minutes), OR (2) Hot water treatment (54°C for 30 minutes), OR (3) Trichoderma-based bioagents (10^8 CFU/ml). This reduces seed-borne fungus by 95%. Ensures disease-free starting material which significantly reduces field infection pressure later. Cost: INR 50-100 per kg of seed.

Nutrient Management

Increase potassium (K) to 60-80 kg/ha to strengthen plant cell walls - resistant tissues slow fungal penetration. Recommended fertilizer ratio: N:P:K = 120:60:60 kg/ha. Add silicon (SiO2) for enhanced resistance. AVOID excess nitrogen which creates soft, vulnerable tissues. Apply nutrient spray of Zinc (0.5%) + Magnesium (0.25%) when disease appears. Deficiency correction cost: INR 1000-2000/acre/season.

Field Sanitation

Burn or bury infected rice straw 20-25cm deep after harvest. Clean irrigation canals and water sources. Use only certified disease-free seeds from reliable sources. Maintain plant spacing 20x20cm for air circulation. Remove weeds competing for light. Sanitize tools between fields. Recovery timeline: 3-4 weeks with treatment.

{% elif prediction.lower() == 'leaf_blast' %}

Fungicide Application

Use Tricyclazole (0.6% TBP at 20-25g/10L water - MOST EFFECTIVE), Propiconazole (0.05%), or Azoxystrobin (0.2% - 20g/10L). Critical timing: Apply at boot stage (panicle initiation - V9 stage) and repeat at heading stage (50% flowering - anthesis). For severe risk, begin spraying 2 weeks into vegetative growth. Standard schedule: 2-3 sprays per season, 10-14 days apart. Cost: INR 400-600 per acre per spray.

Field Management

URGENT: Improve drainage immediately! Blast thrives at RH > 90% and 25-30°C. Reduce standing water - maintain 5cm depth max. Prune lower branches to increase air circulation. Plant spacing critical: 20-25cm between plants. Remove competing weeds that reduce light penetration. Implement raised or sloped beds if field is prone to waterlogging. Night-time cool, humid conditions are dangerous - plan irrigation accordingly.

Resistant Cultivars

Plant approved blast-resistant varieties: Tetep (highly resistant), WAB56-104, IR54, IR64, Basmati, or released resistant lines from your state. Different blast races exist by region - check with local agricultural department for which Pi genes confer resistance. Rotate resistant varieties yearly to prevent race breakdown. Investment: One-time seed cost of INR 200-300 per kg for certified resistant seeds.

Weather Management

Monitor weather forecasts vigilantly during monsoon/high RH periods. High humidity + cool nights = perfect blast conditions. Increase fungicide frequency during rainfall periods. Reduce irrigation frequency during low-risk dry seasons (Jan-April). Record temperature and humidity data - blast risk varies by microclimate. Recovery with treatment: 10-14 days for leaf symptoms, 3-4 weeks for panicle blast impact.

{% elif prediction.lower() == 'leaf_scald' %}

Fungicide Control

Apply Carbendazim (0.1% - 10g per 10L water) OR Mancozeb (0.25% - 25g per 10L water) OR Propiconazole (0.05%). Start spraying from seedling nursery stage if disease history in area. First spray at 4-5 leaf stage (nursery), then repeat every 10-14 days in main field. Use minimum 2-3 sprays per season. Continue until grain filling stage. Cost: INR 250-350 per acre per application. Alternate fungicide classes every 3rd spray to prevent resistance.

Water Sanitation

Use pathogen-free water for ALL irrigation - this is CRITICAL for scald control. Test water source for contamination. Switch from overhead/flood to drip irrigation if possible. Drain fields completely 7-10 days before harvest. For severely contaminated water sources: filter through 0.2µm membrane filter, or UV-treat at 10,000 µW/cm². Standing water: the disease's favorite breeding ground - minimize field flooding. Cost of UV treatment: INR 5000-10000 one-time setup.

Sanitation Protocol

Disinfect ALL equipment (threshers, seeders, harvesters) with 1% bleach solution or 70% ethanol between fields. Remove infected plants immediately - don't mix with healthy material. Source only disease-certified seedlings from reliable nurseries - never use farm-saved seed from infected fields. Quarantine infected field areas. Clean irrigation pipes annually. Document all sanitization activities for future reference.

Prevention

Nursery protection: Grow seedlings in isolated raised beds with protective barriers. Apply preventive fungicides weekly in nursery stage. Inspect seedlings before transplanting - reject any with leaf spotting. Use clean potting mix for nurseries. Recovery timeline: 2-3 weeks for seedling blight, 3-4 weeks for main field infections. Preventive approach is 70% more cost-effective than curative treatment.

{% elif prediction.lower() == 'narrow_brown_spot' %}

Fungicide Treatment

Apply Carbendazim (0.1% - 10g per 10L water), Mancozeb (2 kg/ha or 0.25%), or Tricyclazole (0.6% TBP - 60g per 10L water). Begin spraying when first symptoms appear - don't wait. Continue at 10-14 day intervals. For severe infections, reduce interval to 7 days. Rotate different fungicide groups (triazoles→benzimidazoles→dithiocarbamates) to prevent resistance development. Cost: INR 350-450 per acre per spray. 3-4 sprays typical per season.

Crop Rotation Strategy

AVOID continuous rice cultivation in same field - this allows fungus to accumulate in soil. Implement strict 1-2 year rotation: alternate with wheat, maize, legumes (lentil, chickpea), or vegetables. This breaks the disease cycle by removing host plant. Deeply plow soil to bury infected residue 20-25cm deep (fungus dies in 6-8 months). Use only disease-free seeds and certified seedlings from proven healthy sources - never use farm-saved seed from infected fields.

Soil Health & Nutrition

Incorporate 5-10 tons/hectare organic matter (FYM, compost, crop residue) to enhance natural soil suppressiveness to disease. Maintain optimal soil pH 6.5-7.5 (lime if acidic, sulfur if alkaline). Balanced fertilization: N:P:K = 120:60:60 kg/ha. Avoid nitrogen excess. Add silicon dust or biochar to strengthen plant tissues. Mycorrhizal inoculants improve root health. Cost: INR 8000-15000/acre for organic matter application.

Vigilant Field Monitoring

Inspect plants weekly during entire growing season at multiple field locations (diagonals, corners, edges). Early detection allows intervention before 30% field infection - critical threshold. Scout immediately after rain when humidity high. Keep detailed infection records: date, field location, % infection, which fungicide used - use this to plan next season. Monitor weather: high humidity + warm temps accelerate disease. Recovery timeline: 2-3 weeks for treated fields, 8+ weeks if untreated with potential total crop loss.
